Coping with grief — small steps that matter

❝ Grief is the price we pay for love. Practical, daily practices reduce overwhelm and open space for healing. ❞

Short, research-informed practices to help with day-to-day survival and gradual healing.

1. Create small, manageable routines

Loss disrupts daily structure. Reintroduce tiny routines (morning tea, a short walk) to stabilise sleep and appetite — the foundation for emotional recovery.

2. Name the feeling and allow it

Labeling emotions reduces their intensity. Sit with the feeling for a short period each day instead of pushing it away; this reduces avoidance and promotes processing.

3. Use expressive outlets

Journaling, voice memos, or art can help organize thoughts and preserve memories without becoming overwhelmed.

4. Seek social support and ritual

Simple rituals — lighting a candle, sharing a memory — give meaning. Reach out to one trusted person and ask for specific help (meals, a walk, listening).

5. When to get professional help

If grief impairs daily functioning for months, or you have suicidal thoughts, consult a mental-health professional. Therapies like complicated grief therapy and CBT have strong evidence.
