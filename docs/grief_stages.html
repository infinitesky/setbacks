Understanding common grief stages

❝ Stages are guides, not rules — they help normalize reactions and show there is a path forward. ❞

A compassionate, flexible view of common patterns people experience after loss.

What are 'stages'?

The framework popularized by Elisabeth Kübler‑Ross describes common reactions (denial, anger, bargaining, depression, acceptance). These are not linear rules but useful signposts.

Practical responses at each stage

Respond with small, practical actions: stabilize routines, seek social support, and get professional help when functioning is impaired.
