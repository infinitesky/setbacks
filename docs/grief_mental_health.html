Grief & mental health: when to seek help

❝ Know the signs of complicated grief and where to find evidence-based support. ❞

Guidance on signs of complicated grief and options for support.

Signs of complicated grief

Persistent intense yearning, functional impairment, or prolonged inability to accept the death are red flags for specialized care.

Therapy options

Evidence-based approaches include Complicated Grief Treatment (CGT) and CBT. Online and in-person support groups can also help.

Medication

Antidepressants may be recommended when depression or anxiety accompanies grief; consult a clinician for assessment.

How to find help

Start with primary care, employee assistance programs, or national grief organizations. Ask about therapists trained in grief work.
