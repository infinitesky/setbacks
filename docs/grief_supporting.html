How to support someone who is grieving

❝ Presence matters more than perfection — small acts of care build safety and trust. ❞

Concrete phrases and actions that help more than sympathy alone.

Ask, don't assume

Offer specific help: "May I bring dinner Wednesday?" is easier to accept than "Let me know if you need anything."

Listen more, speak less

Useful phrases: "I'm here," "Tell me about them," or a simple, honest, "I don't know what to say."

Respect timing and boundaries

Grief comes in waves — offer ongoing contact, not only immediately after the loss.

Practical supports

Help with groceries, paperwork, or childcare. Follow through on offers; reliability builds trust.
